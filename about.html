European influence


European influence began when Christopher Columbus arrived in 1492. Most of the islands in the Caribbean was claimed during four of his journeys. During the next few centuries while Europe, Spain, France, Holland, Portugal and Britain where fighting for these new found areas the indigenous people of the Caribbean disappeared and with them most Amerindian visual art also.
European influence is very visible in today's culture, linguistics, religions, politics, academics and sports. The most noteable type of Art seen today is Architecture. Some of the most well known European influenced architecture in the Caribbean are:

  • Port-of-Spain City House of Angostura
  • Port-of-Spain Queen's Royal College
  • Devon House, Kingston
  • St. Nicholas Abbey, Barbados

East Indian influence

East Indians brought with them their religons and cultures. Their art displayed their religous images, clothing, jewllery, musical instruments, tools, utensils and pottery. These East Indians arrived to the Caribbean in indetureship and a system of hired labour.
